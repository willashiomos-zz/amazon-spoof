Not convinced? More stories from our team members:

 

“I used to work for Amazon, both in the warehouse, and at home. I worked in the warehouse for a year, and another year working from home. Something needs to change with Amazon's policies and work environment/conditions. Clearly put, it is modern day slavery that is made legal due to 'grey areas'.

The number one issues I had when working with Amazon at the warehouse was the bathroom to performance issue. Basically, if you wanted to go to the bathroom, you had to worry about getting written up due to your rate going down because depending on where you are in the building (Amazon is a MASSIVE building, with a ton of security measures) you're down 12 minutes of “productivity time” which dramatically affects your rate, and if your rate falls below a certain number, it was an automatic write up without the chance to explain why you went below, it's basically a zero tolerance policy on your rate.

What does this mean for people who work for the warehouse with Amazon? Well, you can starve yourself of water so you don't have to go to the bathroom, or you can risk being written up and/or possibly terminated because of your rate going down due to your break.”

“Let me start off by saying I'm no stranger to hard work, I've done plenty of truly hard working jobs, both physical and mentally. So hard work doesn't scare me, but this is by far the worst I have ever had the misfortune of doing as a job. The back pain that came with this job was grueling, not to mention the number it does on your feet? I would literally come home from work and do nothing but flop on the bed and just lay there… For the year that I survived at the warehouse my life was literally work, bed, work, bed.

It pains me to even say this publicly, but countless times I've thought about committing suicide at the Amazon warehouse facility, there's 3 floors to an Amazon warehouse, and when I was on the third floor, I would sometimes look over the rails and imagine the different ways I could end my life. If it came down to it, I would honestly go homeless first than to go back to working at an Amazon Warehouse.”

“It’s a place where lazy people get promoted, and hard working people get write ups from lazy people. It's a place where they let other employees harass you on a daily basis that includes Management. It's a place where you are set up to fail every single day. You'll never get promoted. You will never get a raise. You'll never get an ounce of respect. You'll get worked like a slave till they find a way to fire you.” .

“I had to pick at 400 units per hour or else I’d get written up. Three write ups is automatic termination. I soon left. Some people love it, I can’t imagine why, because you’re constantly talked down to and walked over like trash unless you mean something to the company and it seems no one is that important unless you’re a fucking robot.”

“I'm not depressed. As far as I can tell, I never have been. Have never thought of suicide (at least in a very serious, genuinely considering it, manner), but by the end of the 4 months before I quit, I contemplated suicide. Like, a lot. It was very scary. I know some people can tough it out and work in these conditions, but I certainly couldn't, and neither could MANY other people. The conditions are so insane, with so little room for context, or improvement, that no one should ever have to work in an Amazon Warehouse. FUCK Bezos and his stupid ass introduction video telling me how fucking great he is and how much I'm going to love Amazon―I did not.”
